## Company Overview

**What the company does:** 
Chick-fil-A, Inc. is an American fast food restaurant chain and the largest chain specializing in chicken sandwiches

their specialty "original chicken sandwich" - a piece of deep-fried breaded boneless chicken breast served on a toasted bun with two slices of dill pickle, or with lettuce, tomato, and cheese

**Size and Scale:** 
In 2024, Chick-fil-A achieved total systemwide sales of $22.746 billion

the third-largest restaurant chain in the U.S. by sales, alongside McDonald's ($53.5 billion) and Starbucks ($30.4 billion)

3,109 locations, compared to McDonald's 13,559 U.S. locations

the highest average unit volume at $7.5 million per location among major U.S. fast-food chains

**Revenue Performance:** 
Revenue topped $9 billion in 2024 compared to $7.8 billion in 2023, an increase of 15.3 percent

The company's non-mall locations generated average unit revenues of $9.4 million in 2024

approximately 34 percent of restaurants having annual sales volumes exceeding $10 million, and the highest-volume location topping $19 million

**Headquarters and Geographic Presence:** 
Headquartered in College Park, Georgia, Chick-fil-A operates restaurants across 48 states, as well as in the District of Columbia and Puerto Rico
. The company also has 
operations in Canada and the United Kingdom

**Ownership Structure:** 
Chick-fil-A, Inc. is a family-owned business, founded by S. Truett Cathy

The company has always been a privately held, family-owned entity. This structure means the company has never had an IPO, thus no public shareholders, institutional investors, or mutual funds own any part of it

**Leadership:** 
Today, Truett's children—Dan T. Cathy, Donald (Bubba) M. Cathy and Trudy Cathy White—serve as Chairman, Executive Vice President and President, Dwarf House, and Ambassador, respectively. Andrew Cathy, Truett's grandson, serves as CEO

Susannah Frost was named President in August 2024, making her the sixth person to hold the leadership role since the company's founding in 1967
